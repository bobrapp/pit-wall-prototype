Analyst · Workbench Demo
Silverado HD Override — Gate 3 Blocked
Walk through an analyst resolving a Second Opinion challenge. The AI has flagged a 6.1% divergence on Silverado HD 1500 and Gate 3 is blocked pending the analyst's decision.
App: Analyst Workbench (Lovable)
Persona: Krisztina G. · Analyst 1
Goal: Unblock Gate 3 via override + HIBT commit
Launch App →
1
Open the Analyst Workbench
The dashboard loads with a "You Were Right" toast for Silverado HD 2500 — point out the HIBT reference number and Championship points awarded automatically.
↗ Open Dashboard
Talk Track
"Notice the instant confirmation — the system tracked Krisztina's C4 override, compared it to actuals when they arrived, and auto-awarded Championship points. No manual follow-up."
2
Review the action items on Dashboard
Two urgent items are pinned at the top — "Second Opinion: Silverado HD 1500" (MDS 0.72, Gate 3 blocked) and "Second Opinion: Equinox EV" (MDS 0.61). Click either one to jump to Second Opinion.
Gate 3 Blocked
💡 Point out the MDS badge next to each action item — it quantifies how strongly the AI disagrees, not just that it disagrees.
3
Navigate to Second Opinion tab
Click "Second Opinion" in the sidebar (or click "Review SO" from the Forecasts table). The MDS 0.72 challenge for Silverado HD is expanded by default showing all 4 divergent factors.
↗ Second Opinion Tab
Talk Track
"Each factor is explained in plain language. Tariff feed stale — 3 days. Fleet/retail mix divergence. Ford F-150 competitive pressure. The 35%/28%/22%/15% weights show how much each factor drove the AI's bear case."
4
Click "Submit Revised Override"
The override form slides open. Edit the volume (try 278,500) and the confidence dropdown. The reasoning textarea is pre-filled — show how it captures dealer visit confirmation and tariff data acknowledgement.
HIBT Commit
Talk Track
"This reasoning text becomes immutable. In 18 months, if Silverado actuals come in and someone asks 'why did we make that call?', the HIBT reference answers it — with the exact text Krisztina wrote today."
5
Click "Submit to HIBT"
The challenge card updates to "Override Submitted", Gate 3 unblocks, and the override appears at the top of Inbox with a HIBT reference number. A toast notification confirms the commit.
Talk Track
"Notice the Inbox — the submission created a new notification automatically. The HIBT reference is a cryptographic record. If Krisztina leaves the team next year, this call is still attributed to her reasoning."
6
(Optional) Open AI Chat and ask about the gap to #2
Switch to AI Chat, type "How many points do I need to reach #2?" — the AI responds with the exact gap (14 pts), names the current holder, and suggests which override scenarios could close it.
Championship
Talk Track
"The Championship meta-game is optional, but it surfaces something real: analysts who take more accurate overrides climb the table. Accuracy and engagement become the same metric."

Leadership · Executive Dashboard Demo
Cycle 5 S&OP Review — One-Click PPT
Walk a VP through the executive dashboard. Cycle health at a glance, team performance breakdown, Championship standings, and exporting a ready-to-present S&OP deck in one click.
App: Leadership Dashboard (Figma Make)
Persona: VP NAMA / S&OP Sponsor
Goal: Assess cycle health and export PPT for executive meeting
Launch App →
1
Open the Leadership Dashboard
The Cycle Health tab loads immediately with the hero DQ score (87/100, green) and a Cycle Pulse strip showing all 6 DQ dimensions. No login, no filters — executive-ready by default.
↗ Open Dashboard
Talk Track
"This is read-only. A VP opens this the morning of S&OP and gets one number: 87. Green. The cycle is in good shape. No digging required unless they want to drill down."
2
Scroll to Nameplate Health grid
Six vehicle nameplates with individual DQ scores. Equinox EV is in amber (72) — tariff feed risk. Silverado HD is green (91). Point out that each card shows the human owner and pending actions.
Talk Track
"Equinox EV at 72 — below the green threshold. The VP can see at a glance that there are 2 pending actions. They don't need to know the technical reason, just that Mansoor's team has it covered."
3
Click "Team Performance" tab
Rolling 6-cycle MAPE leaderboard. Krisztina leads at 3.1% mean error. Show the column sparklines and point out that the system tracks who's improving cycle-over-cycle.
Talk Track
"This is non-judgmental visibility. Leadership isn't using this to evaluate individuals — they're using it to identify where the team needs support or model improvement."
4
Click "Export" tab — hit "Export PowerPoint"
The button transitions through "Generating…" to "✓ Ready — Download" in about 2.5 seconds. Point out the slide preview thumbnails that populate to show what's in the deck.
Talk Track
"Before this, someone spent 45 minutes on a Monday morning copying DQ scores into a PowerPoint. Now it's one click. The slides are auto-populated from the same data the analysts just worked with."
5
(Optional) Toggle Present Mode
Click "Present Mode" in the top bar. The UI scales up 20%, the topbar hides, and the dashboard fills the screen — projector-ready. Press Escape to exit. Useful when presenting directly from a browser.
💡 Present Mode works best on a wide screen or projector. All tooltips and hover interactions still work in present mode.

Engineering · GovOps Sandbox Demo
DQ Weight Audit + HIBT Determinism Check
Walk a GovOps engineer or SME through tuning the DQ engine weight configuration, exploring the HIBT ledger, and running a replay determinism check on a historical override.
App: Engineering Sandbox (Replit)
Persona: GovOps Engineer / AI SME
Goal: Tune DQ weights, verify HIBT replay determinism
Launch App →
1
Open the Engineering Sandbox — DQ Tuner tab
Six weight sliders for the DQ dimensions. The current weight total is shown in red if over-allocated (sum > 100%). Point out the formula at the bottom recalculating live as sliders move.
↗ Open DQ Tuner
Talk Track
"DQ weights are a governance decision — GOV-005 defines the approval workflow. This tool lets engineers simulate 'what if we weighted freshness more heavily?' before submitting a change request."
2
Drag Freshness slider from 25% to 35%
Watch the four preview scores recalculate instantly. Equinox EV's score drops (it has a stale tariff feed) while Silverado HD holds steady. The total goes above 100% — point out the red warning.
Live Recalc
Talk Track
"This immediately shows the blast radius. Boosting freshness weight would turn Equinox EV red — alerting the team it needs a data refresh before the cycle is certified."
3
Click "HIBT Ledger" tab — filter by OVERRIDE
Use the Action dropdown to filter to OVERRIDE entries. The ledger narrows to show only analyst overrides with their HIBT IDs, timestamps, surfaces, and reasons.
↗ Open HIBT Ledger Immutable Ledger
Talk Track
"Every row has a surface column — was this submitted from Slack, Teams, Workbench, or API? That's how the system maintains surface neutrality. Same data quality regardless of where the analyst worked."
4
Click "▶ Replay" on the Silverado HD OVERRIDE row
The replay runs a 4-step terminal animation: loading snapshot → reconstructing payload → re-running DQ engine → diff comparison. Shows determinism percentage plus a field-level diff of what diverged.
Determinism Check
Talk Track
"98.2% determinism — 4 values differ. That's the system telling us: if you re-ran this exact override from the same HIBT snapshot today, you'd get a nearly identical result. The small delta is from non-deterministic random seeds in the DQ engine — documented in GOV-001."
5
Switch to MDS Calculator — try a synthetic override
Navigate to the MDS tab. Enter: Analyst Forecast 310,000, Primary Model 280,000, Confidence 0.8. The MDS score calculates to ~0.54 (above 0.30 threshold — would trigger Gate 3 block).
Talk Track
"This is the sandbox version of what happens automatically in the analyst's workbench. Here, engineers can tune the MDS formula inputs before a model change gets deployed."
6
(Optional) Switch Surface Adapter chips
Click through the surface chips (Slack → Teams → Outlook → Excel). Each shows the sync latency, last sync time, and whether the surface is primary or secondary for that dimension. Slack shows real-time, Excel shows 24h batch.
💡 The surface latency differences are architecturally significant — a forecast confirmed in Slack routes through the streaming adapter, while Excel uses the overnight batch sync. Both land in the same HIBT ledger.
